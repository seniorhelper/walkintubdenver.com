Frequently Asked Questions

Denver Walk In Tub FAQs

These are the questions we hear most from Denver and Colorado homeowners. If you want quick clarity, start here.

How much does a walk in tub cost in Denver?
Pricing depends on the tub size, features selected, and installation needs. In general, walk in tub projects in Denver range from entry-level units to fully loaded hydrotherapy configurations. The fastest way to get accurate Denver pricing is to request a quote with your ZIP code and basic bathroom details. We will follow up with real numbers, not a range designed to get you on the phone.
How long does installation take?
In most Denver-area homes, professional installation is completed in a single day. Our factory-certified installers remove the existing tub, prepare the space, install the new walk in tub, connect plumbing, and do a full cleanup — all in one visit. In some cases with unusual plumbing configurations or older subfloor conditions, a second day may be needed, but that is the exception rather than the rule.
Can I use my own contractor for installation?
Yes. Our DIY drop-ship program lets you purchase your chosen walk in tub and have it shipped directly to your home or your contractor's location. You still receive full white glove product guidance and sizing help from us — and the full product warranty applies. Your contractor simply handles the physical installation. We provide spec sheets and are available to answer technical questions during the project.
How do I know what size walk in tub fits my bathroom?
We look at your layout, doorway clearance, existing tub footprint, and plumbing locations. Then we recommend sizes that fit cleanly and function well. Most standard Denver bathrooms can accommodate a walk in tub within the existing tub alcove. If your bathroom has an unusual layout, we work through the options with you before any purchase is made.
Is a transfer-style tub better than a standard walk in tub?
It depends on your mobility profile and bathing routine. A transfer-style walk in tub has a door on the long side of the tub, which makes entering from a wheelchair or mobility aid easier. A standard walk in tub has a door on the end and is more suitable for people who can step in and prefer a traditional tub feel. We help you evaluate which entry style matches your current needs and your likely needs over the next several years.
What features are actually worth paying for?
The features most consistently rated as worthwhile by our clients are hydrotherapy jets for joint and muscle relief, a heated seat for comfort during fill and drain time, a fast drain system to reduce waiting in a cooling tub, and a handheld showerhead for rinsing. Air bubble systems, chromotherapy lighting, and aromatherapy are popular upgrades but less universally essential. We help you evaluate which features match your specific comfort goals so you only pay for what you'll actually use.
Do walk in tubs require a lot of hot water?
Walk in tubs do use more hot water than a standard shower because you enter before the tub fills. Most Denver homes with a standard 40–50 gallon water heater can support a walk in tub, though you may notice the second fill of the day is less warm than the first. Households with tankless water heaters have no issue. We review your hot water situation as part of our guidance process so there are no surprises after installation.
Do you service areas outside Denver?
Yes — we serve all of Colorado. Our professional installation team covers the entire Denver metro area including Boulder, Fort Collins, Colorado Springs, Pueblo, and the surrounding Front Range. We also offer drop-ship service to all 64 Colorado counties, including the Western Slope, mountain communities, and the Eastern Plains. See our Areas We Serve page for more detail.
Is there a warranty on the walk in tub?
Yes. The products we carry come with manufacturer warranties covering the tub shell, jets, and mechanical components. Additionally, when you choose our professional installation service, the installation itself is warranted. We walk through all warranty details with you during our guidance process so you understand exactly what is covered and for how long before you commit to a purchase.
Can a walk in tub help with arthritis or joint pain?
Many of our clients report significant relief from soaking in warm water with hydrotherapy jets, particularly for arthritis, hip pain, back stiffness, and leg circulation issues. While we are not able to make medical claims, warm water immersion combined with targeted hydrotherapy has a well-documented history as a comfort therapy for joint conditions. If this is a primary motivation for your purchase, we can specifically focus on hydrotherapy jet configurations that target the areas you care most about.
What is the difference between air jets and hydrotherapy jets?
Air jets push thousands of tiny air bubbles through the water, creating a gentle, effervescent sensation across the whole body. Hydrotherapy (or water) jets push pressurized water through targeted nozzles, providing a more focused massage-like sensation on specific muscle groups. Many walk in tubs offer both systems, which can be used independently or together. We help you understand which combination fits your comfort goals and physical needs.
Can I get a VA HISA grant to help pay for a walk in tub?
Veterans may qualify for the VA Home Improvements and Structural Alterations (HISA) grant, which can cover accessible bathing modifications including walk in tubs. Eligibility depends on service-related disability status and other factors. We have experience working with Veterans and can provide the documentation your contractor or VA representative needs. Visit Aging Safely Baths' VA HISA guide for complete eligibility information.
How do I prepare my bathroom before installation day?
We provide a simple preparation checklist once your installation is scheduled. In general, you'll want to clear the path from your front door to the bathroom, remove personal items from the bathroom surfaces, and ensure the water shutoff valves are accessible. Our installation team brings all tools and materials — you do not need to purchase anything in advance. We handle the old tub removal and disposal as part of the service.
What happens if I have a problem after installation?

If you experience any issue with the tub or the installation — whether it's a mechanical question, a comfort adjustment, or a warranty claim — we are a phone call away.
